November 2025 – January 2026

November 20, 2025 — Equipment & Direction

  • The Anytone AT-D878 appears to be a strong choice: capable in both analog and DMR, with repeaters in range for each.
  • HF does not feel like a practical starting point for a hobby I don’t yet know I enjoy.
  • DMR feels like a good compromise: real DX potential without HF infrastructure.
  • Unsure how much I actually want to talk versus listen.
  • Satellite work (ISS) alone feels sufficient to justify the hobby.
  • HTs appear capable of satellite work with simple antennas.
  • Important to match digital mode to local repeaters; DMR appears best supported locally.
  • Buying an analog-only radio in 2025 feels unnecessarily limiting.

Dad’s note: He once worked outside Iowa from a 2m/70cm base station with a vertical—rare, but possible.

November 30, 2025 — Radio Purchased

  • Few C4FM repeaters nearby; not expecting initial success there.
  • Purchased Anytone 878 II (Bluetooth) — not opening until licensed / Christmas.
  • Avoided cheap gear to eliminate uncertainty over equipment quality.
  • Best explanation of amateur radio to non-hams: bands as slices of the electromagnetic spectrum, similar to cellular carriers.
  • Feels as much about the art of communication as communication itself.

December 3, 2025 — Technician Exam

  • Took Technician exam online through PARC and passed.

December 15, 2025 — CW

  • Began learning Morse using Morse Mania.
  • Surprisingly accessible via an app.

December 21, 2025 — Preparation Phase

  • Reading the radio manual and watching programming videos on YouTube.
  • Reviewing Bergen FM Association repeaters.
  • Managing expectations: activity may be sparse.
  • Plan: listen before transmitting.

December 25, 2025 — First Breakthroughs

  • Indoor reception initially quiet.
  • Installed ¼-wave whip antenna.
  • NOAA Weather Radio received loud and clear (analog, reduced squelch).
  • First repeater heard: 442.9 MHz Alpine, NJ (~10–15 miles).
  • Christmas night net run by an elderly operator—unexpectedly meaningful.
  • Motivation significantly increased.

December 26, 2025 — Nets & Reality

  • Listened to NWS Skywarn net during snowfall.
  • Weather events are ideal entry points.
  • Heard deliberate interference on a repeater (heckling, old radio broadcasts).
  • Reminder that radio culture includes everything.

December 27, 2025 — Jupiter, Florida

  • Picked up Jupiter repeater (~1 mile away).
  • Manually programmed offset and CTCSS into a “FL” zone.
  • Not intuitive, but manageable without a computer.
  • Digital complexity adds setup burden, though scanning functions normally.
  • NOAA Weather Radio test:
    • Outside: very clear
    • Inside: completely dead
  • Continued listening before transmitting.

December 28, 2025 — First Attempts

  • Listened to nationwide AllStar net.
  • Attempted check-in; not heard.
  • Suspected TX programming issue.
  • Confirmed error: TX frequency programmed incorrectly.
  • Lesson reinforced: small details matter.
  • Key milestone: comfortable pressing PTT.

December 29, 2025 — DMR Basics

  • Clarified need for a DMR ID to access public DMR repeaters.
  • Registered for ID.
  • Planned retry on local DMR repeater.

December 30, 2025 — Florida DMR

  • Jupiter–Tequesta group net received on both repeaters.
  • Same net running on both time slots simultaneously.
  • Attempted DMR texting with Dad—unsuccessful.

January 1, 2026 — Mixed Results

  • Attempted Jupiter repeater using Signal Stick from second floor indoors — no success.

January 3, 2026 — Massachusetts

  • Attempted W1BIM nightly net.
  • RX solid from second floor; TX unsuccessful.
  • Received signals from ~45 miles away, exceeding expectations.
  • Nets feel like structured Zoom meetings—on RF.
  • Began building codeplug; exported repeater data from RepeaterBook → CSV → CPS.
  • Loaded 2–3 dozen repeaters quickly.
  • Prudential Building repeater: strong RX, TX uncertain.

January 4, 2026 — First DMR Transmission

  • While troubleshooting Pi-based DMR setup, accidentally kerchunked.
  • Signal returned.
  • Repeater located in Marshfield (~30 miles).
  • Confirmed activity via BrandMeister dashboard.
  • First successful transmission — DMR.

January 5, 2026 — Practicality

  • Bluetooth allows HT placement upstairs while listening downstairs.
  • Radio supports short, incremental sessions (10 minutes at a time).

January 6, 2026 — Antenna Upgrade

  • Ordered Slim Jim 2m/70cm window antenna.
  • Best compromise short of permanent installation.
  • Expectations raised after long-distance RX success.
  • Purchased Raspberry Pi 3B for hotspot use.

January 8, 2026 — Boston Attempt

  • Called Boston repeater requesting signal report.
  • No voice response; repeater sent CW ID.
  • Possibly too weak or garbled.
  • Second-floor window, repeater ~25 miles away at ~750 ft elevation.
  • Terrain obstruction (hill) suspected.

January 10, 2026 — Radio Organization

  • AT-878 zones, channels, and scan lists initially awkward but logical.
  • Enables different operating contexts:
    • Local FM
    • Local DMR
    • NJ
    • MA
    • Travel
  • Worth the complexity if you value structure and scale.

January 11, 2026 — DMR Depth

  • Talkgroups add an additional organizational layer.
  • Multiple TGs on the same repeater require separate channels.
  • Assembled Pi-Star hotspot.

January 12, 2026 — Pi-Star Fix

  • Root cause identified: DMR ID required “01” suffix.
  • System functioned immediately after correction.

January 13, 2026 — First QSO

  • BrandMeister Hoseline proved valuable for learning.
  • Internet-backed, but intentionally curated.
  • First international QSO with China.
  • Notable given Three-Body Problem inspiration.
  • UHF easily links upstairs HT to basement Pi.

January 15, 2026 — Slim Jim Installed

  • Slim Jim window-mounted.
  • Easily worked W1BIM for nightly Elmers’ old-timers net.
  • Trivia question missed: steam-powered device patented in 1861 → elevator.
  • Fixed antenna setup feels more “real” than handheld positioning.

January 17, 2026 — New Jersey Nets

  • Found nightly NJ statewide linked net.
  • Local repeater: Alpine, NJ (~13 miles) across the Hudson.
  • Slim Jim clipped to hanger in window.
