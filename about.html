about

I came to amateur radio in late 2024 after reading Remembrance of Earth’s Past by Liu Cixin and listening to the podcast Daniel and Kelly’s Extraordinary Universe. Both renewed my curiosity about how the universe works. Amateur radio is a hands-on way to explore that curiosity through the electromagnetic spectrum.

Through elementary, middle, and high school I studied math, eventually taking 2–3 years of calculus and assuming it would continue to come easily. When it got too hard, I burned out and moved on. Now I’m returning to that foundation, reconnecting with what I learned and approaching it more deliberately.

I operate VHF/UHF and DMR with a Technician license using a 5-watt handheld. I’m working toward my Extra license. Along the way, I’ll be exploring HF operation, FT8, antenna experiments, basic Python, and taking a hands-on approach to learning about radio and the physics behind it.

This site documents the learning process.

short and long term goals

This is a hobby with a million rabbit holes and I'm interested in a lot of them. I'd like to be able to track my learning progress. After a few months in the hobby, these my goals for 2026-2027:

  • Make 200-300 QSOs
  • Find a weekly net / ham club to participate in
  • Build a homemade 2m yagi and use it to work the ISS
  • Pass the General and Amateur Extra exams
  • Dust off high school calculus and use it to eventually work up to understanding Maxwell's equations
  • Read (and thoroughly understand) Ron Bertrand’s Radio Theory Handbook and David J. Griffiths' Introduction to Electrodynamics
  • Continue to develop website and develop Python scripts
