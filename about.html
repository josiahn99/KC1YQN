about

I came to amateur radio in late 2024 after reading Remembrance of Earth’s Past by Liu Cixin and listening to the podcast Daniel and Kelly’s Extraordinary Universe. Both renewed my curiosity about how the universe works. Amateur radio is a hands-on way to explore that curiosity through the electromagnetic spectrum.

Through elementary, middle, and high school I studied math, eventually taking 2–3 years of calculus and assuming it would continue to come easily. When it got too hard, I burned out and moved on. Now I’m returning to that foundation, reconnecting with what I learned and approaching it more deliberately.

I operate VHF/UHF and DMR with a Technician license using a 5-watt handheld. Taking a hands-on approach, I'm learning the phsyics of radio and documenting the journey on this site.

short and long term goals

This is a hobby with a million rabbit holes and I'm interested in a lot of them. To stay focused and track my learning, these are my goals for 2026-2027:

  • Make 200-300 QSOs
  • Find a weekly net / ham club to participate in
  • Build a homemade 2m yagi and use it to work the ISS and distant repeaters
    • Model the Yagi in software
    • Compare predicted vs real gain
    • Measure SWR bandwidth
  • Pass the General and Amateur Extra exams
  • Work toward's understanding Maxwell's equations in David J. Griffith's Introduction to Electrodynamics
    • Dust off single-variable calculus
    • Complete multi-variable calc online course
    • Complete a vector calc online course
  • Read (and thoroughly understand) Ron Bertrand’s Radio Theory Handbook
  • Continue to develop website, Raspberry Pi experimentation and develop Python scripts
  • Conduct basic experiments in HF and electrical theory
  • Set up a full Dx ham shack and base station operating on 20m and 40m, building and modeling the antenna
Some of these are more practical than others. The ham shack is certainly the primary goal. Maxwell's equations, I'm a bit nervous I'm biting off way too much. But it's important to me to try.
